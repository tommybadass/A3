Synopsis

"Catch Me If You Can" is a 2002 biographical crime film directed by Steven Spielberg, based on the life of Frank Abagnale Jr., who, before his 19th birthday, successfully performed cons worth millions of dollars by posing as a Pan Am pilot, a doctor, and a lawyer. The film stars Leonardo DiCaprio as Abagnale, Tom Hanks as the FBI agent pursuing him, and Christopher Walken as his father. The film is known for its dynamic storytelling and strong performances, reflecting the real-life escapades of one of the most famous con artists in history.
